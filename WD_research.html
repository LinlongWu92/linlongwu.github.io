Waveform Design for Next-Generation Radar Systems

   

{}{img_left}{photosWD_page_overview.jpg}{Waveform_design}{525px}{250px} The essence of radar systems across various applications is to swiftly and accurately perceive the environment, a capability that is directly and significantly influenced by the transmitted waveform. Advancements in radar hardware, such as ADCsDACs, DSPs, and antenna technology, have made the implementation of complex waveforms feasible, pushing the boundaries of what radar systems can achieve.

Designing effective waveforms involves a multi-dimensional, multi-domain approach, considering parameters such as amplitude, phase, frequency, time, polarization, code, and spatial characteristics. This approach seeks optimal solutions within a feasible set defined by hardware limitations, application scenarios, and user-defined preferences. By leveraging these degrees of freedom (DoF), radar waveform design addresses complex challenges and enables innovative solutions that enhance radar capabilities among various applications.

Towards the future, leveraging large datasets and machine learning to optimize waveforms, integrating deep learning with traditional signal processing for real-time optimization, and combining different sensing modalities will create more robust and versatile systems. This evolution will enable next-generation radar systems to meet modern demands, ensuring robust, adaptive, and high-performance capabilities across various fields.
